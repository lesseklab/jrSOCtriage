# Support Contracts

Paid support for jrSOCtriage is available directly from the person who
built it.

If you want help getting jrSOCtriage running well in your environment —
deploying it, tuning it, and integrating it with your existing SOC stack —
a support contract gives you direct access to the author for exactly that.

## Free community support

Before anything paid: basic help is free. If you hit a bug, have a
question, or want to request a feature, open an issue on the jrSOCtriage
GitHub repository:

**[GitHub Issues — jrSOCtriage](https://github.com/lesseklab/jrSOCtriage/issues)**

Community support is best-effort and handled as time allows, but it costs
nothing and it's the right first stop for most questions. A support
contract (below) is for when you want direct, hands-on, deployment-specific
help rather than waiting on a public issue thread.

## What a support contract covers

Support is best-effort and scoped to what you actually need. Typical
engagements include:

- **Deployment and setup** — installing jrSOCtriage, getting the pipeline
  running against your Wazuh (and Suricata) alert sources, and confirming
  it's healthy.
- **Configuration and tuning** — dialing in rule levels, thresholds,
  dedup behavior, escalation rules, and notification settings for your
  environment and alert volume.
- **SOC stack integration** — connecting jrSOCtriage to the tools you
  already run: Graylog, Zeek, ntopng, your local or cloud LLM endpoints,
  and your notification paths.
- **Troubleshooting** — working through issues, unexpected behavior, or
  questions as they come up.

## Scoped to your deployment

Support is scoped to your situation, which is why there's no fixed price.
Helping a single-instance deployment is a very different engagement from
helping roll out multiple instances across a custom, multi-site, or
multi-tenant environment. Whatever your setup, support is scoped — and
priced — to what you're actually running.

## Best-effort, direct from the author

Support is best-effort. jrSOCtriage is built and maintained by one
person, so there's no 24/7 help desk and no guaranteed response time —
what you get instead is direct access to the person who wrote the
software, who understands it end to end. I'll do my genuine best to help
and to respond promptly, but I can't promise immediate turnaround at all
times. If you need a formal, guaranteed-SLA enterprise support
arrangement, jrSOCtriage support in its current form may not be the right
fit — and I'd rather tell you that up front than overpromise.

## Support is separate from a commercial license

A support contract and a commercial license are two different things.

Commercial *use* of jrSOCtriage — for example, using it to provide
managed services, MSSP/MDR/SOC offerings, or any other service to third
parties — requires a separate commercial license, as described in the
jrSOCtriage license. A support contract does **not** grant any commercial
rights.

Support contracts are available for any deployment, whether you're using
jrSOCtriage for your own internal operations (no commercial license
required) or under a commercial license. If you need both a commercial
license and support, get in touch and we can cover both in the same
conversation.
